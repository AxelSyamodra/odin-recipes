Rendang Omelette from Scrambled on the Road

Gordon Ramsay’s Rendang Omelette from Scrambled on the Road is a vibrant fusion of Indonesian-inspired flavors and classic egg mastery. This unique dish combines the rich, aromatic spices of traditional beef rendang with the delicate texture of a perfectly cooked omelette. The rendang mixture, slow-cooked to tender perfection, is packed with fragrant ingredients like lemongrass, galangal, coconut milk, and a medley of warming spices that infuse the beef with deep, savory flavor. Ramsay balances the boldness of the rendang by folding it into soft, fluffy eggs, creating a rich yet harmonious combination.

The omelette itself is cooked gently to achieve a smooth, creamy texture, with the rendang filling adding bursts of spice and richness in every bite. Ramsay often garnishes the dish with fresh herbs like cilantro and thinly sliced red chilies for a vibrant kick. The result is a beautifully balanced plate that combines the comforting warmth of rendang with the light, airy texture of an expertly cooked omelette — a true showcase of Ramsay’s skill in blending bold global flavors with refined cooking techniques.

Ingredients

  • 4 Shallots, Thinly Sliced
  • 3 Garlic Cloves, Thinly Sliced
  • 2 Inches of Ginger Root, Thinly Sliced
  • 2 Red Chilis, Chopped
  • Oil (Canola or Grapeseed)
  • Kaffir Lime Leaves
  • 1 1 Inch Slice of Galangal (Available at Speciality and Asian Grocery Stores)
  • 4-6 dried whitebait (or your favorite dried fish), chopped
  • 4 Duck Eggs (or use regular eggs as a substitute)
  • 1 Teaspoon of Red Chili Paste
  • 1 Teaspoon of Green Chili Paste

Steps

  1. Add about a tablespoon of oil to a pan and once its hot add the garlic, shallots, chili and ginger. Add a few kaffir lime leaves as well. After about a minute at the galangal. Once the galangal is in chop the fish and add to the mixture.
  2. Once garlic and shallots are caramelized, take out the kaffir leaves and galangal out.
  3. Crack eggs into a bowl and mix. Add chili paste mixes until combined. Once fully combined, add to garlic and shallot mixture. Mix egg mixture with the shallot mixture until eggs are two-thirds of the way cooked. Once they are two-thirds cooked, the flatten the full mixture on the pan and let cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add rendang mixture to top of the eggs as they cook, putting an even amount across the egg mixture (rendang recipe below!).
  5. As the eggs cook, tap the pan so the eggs are released from the bottom of the pan. As eggs are fully cooked, tap pan 3 times and tilt. Using a spatula start from the top of the tilted pan and start to fold the eggs over. Continue to tip the pan as you fold until it gets to the bottom of the pan where you can roll it straight to your plate!
